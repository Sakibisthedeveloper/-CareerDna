PATTERN 1 — The "Th" to "G" / "Ch" Shift:
  Front dental sounds like "The" or "Through" consistently move toward back-palatal or velar regions, producing sound roots like "ga-" or "choo".
PATTERN 2 — The "B/Br" Nasal Shift:
  Initial Br or B sounds at the start of a word or phrase soften into a nasal/vowel combination (Me-aw, Me-ah, Mish, Men, Me-).
PATTERN 3 — Initial S-Cluster Prefix:
  Words starting with S combined with a hard consonant (strikes, stain, split, stars, sky) trigger a soft initial vowel placeholder (is-arts, is-tain, is-plit, es-tas, es-kai).
PATTERN 4 — Unvoiced Plosive / Dropped Final Consonant:
  Final trailing hard d, g, x sounds transition to unvoiced t or drop completely (good → oot, dog → fob, fox → soff, wind → wine, jumped → jam).
PATTERN 5 — Sibilant / Friction Reduction:
  Words starting with an "S" blend use an introductory air vowel to push the friction forward (stars → es-tas, sky → es-kai, start → es-tat).
PATTERN 6 — Compounding Suffix Rule:
  Words ending in -thing or -down sometimes pick up an extra placeholder syllable before the word ends (everything → en-thing, locked down → lock-rah-own).
